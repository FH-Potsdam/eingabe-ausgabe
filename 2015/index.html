Algorithm

The task was to create an algorithm for humans and execute it several times. Iterate through the results and refine the pseudo-code. Afterwards the algorithm was ported to the Processing programming language and improved. Finally the students needed to find a use case for their algorithms.

Slime Mold

This thing not animal not plant has a will of his own. The task was to explore the possibilities of interacting with this creature. Can its behavior trigger an input? Using openCV we analyzed how this little creeper interacts with his environment

Motor Poster

The aim was to develop a human computer interaction that takes a passive input. No button press no touching. Just the presence of a person in front of the object is enough to trigger an event.
